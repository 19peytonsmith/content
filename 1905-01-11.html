        3.14. PORT OF ALEXANDRIA.

        We are glad to see that the London newspapers are beginning to take up the question of dock deficiency at Alexandria, and Mr. Cornish's article on the subject has been quoted at home.

        The "Standard" of December 31st, in the course of some oomments on the subject from its Manchester correspondent, remarks :—

        In the course of an interview yesterday with a representative of the firm of Messrs. Gough and Crosthwaite, who are among the largest shippers from Manchester to Egypt, our correspondent was informed that there had been, and were now, delays in the discharging of cargoes at Alexandria, owing to the lack of proper dock space. In some instances the delays were very serious, especially in the case of vessels which had to proceed up the Syrian Coast alter leaving Alexandria. The shipments to the last-named port were steadily growing, the increase in the last ten years having been somewhere about fifty per cent, and it was absolutely necessary, in the interests of the country generally, that steps should be taken as promptly as possible for affording proper facilities for loading and unloading boats with despatch. He was greatly pleased to find that the International Chamber of Shipping had taken up the question, and hoped it would be pushed forward with vigour.
