        (From our Special Correspondent).

        Port Said, Tuesday.

        The long-expected squadron has at last arrived here.         A considerable number of people gathered along the front to watch the passage of the ships, and many cameras were in evidence, the probability of most of these ships being sent to the bottom before long no doubt acting as an incentive to collect souvenirs.

        A large number of Russian spies have been noticed in the town for some days. Weird, unkempt-looking individuals, they loaf about the cafés in an aimless way, day and night, doing nothing, for there is certainly not the slightest work for them to do here. Precautionary measures have, it is true, been taken by the authorities ; but not to the same elaborate extent as before, and the spirit of the officers in charge of these duties is that of an amused participator in a harmless pantomine.

        The first vessels of the fleet to enter the harbour were two of the destroyers, followed closely by the flagship "Oreg.” Another destroyer followed, then came the second-class cruiser "Izumrund,” followed by the Volunteer Fleet ships "Rion” and ‘‘Dnieper.’’ These last two vessels are, respectively, our old friends the "Smolensk" and “Petersburg,” which passed the Dardanelles under the merchant and Red Cross flags and seized, amongst other ships, the P. and O. steamer “Malacca.” The new names they bear, and the coat of dark grey paint, are no doubt a concession to England’s vigorous protest, followed by the demonstration made in these waters in July last by eight battleships of our Mediterranean Squadron, on account of the breach of the Treaty of Paris.

        The "Rion” and “Dnieper” look smart and well-kept, but the “Oreg” and “Izumrud,” notwithstanding the fact that they are comparatively new ships, look rather seedy, and do not strike one as having been in dry-dock of late.

        3.11. CAIRO TOURIST SEASON.
        PROMISES TO BE A RECORD ONE

        We are informed that the managers of the hotels in the Capital, as well as at Helouan, Mena House, and up-river, are jubilant at the large number and class of tourists that are already crowding all the winter resorts of Egypt, and there are unmistakable signs in all directions that the present will prove to be the record season, surpassing even the last, which was considered the best so far.

        As an instance, we may say that at one of the leading Cairo hotels,—the Grand Continental,—there was a daily average during the month of November of no less than 120 visitora more than during the corresponding month of the previous year, and for December the daily number was even greater. Shepheard’s has been crowded for weeks, and the other fashionable hostelries, the Savoy and Ghezireh Palace Hotels, are also filling up rapidly.

        The smaller hotels everywhere report the same good news, and the managers aver that never before have their houses been full so early, despite the large number of tourists that have already left for the Nile trip and Khartoum.

        3.15. MINISTRY OF PUBLIC INSTRUCTION.

        We would desire to draw attention to the prominence given to the Ministry of Public instruction in the Note recently published by the Financial Adviser. The budget for 1905 has been increased by L.E 31,300 over that of 1904. This is a gratifying feature and, with out doubt, will tend towards a general improvement of the education of the country. Hitherto young educated Egyptians have found it more profitable to pursue their higher studies for qualification in Medicine, Law, and the more liberal professions. Teaching as a profession did not seem to offer many adequate prizes ; but we have no hesitation in stating that a perusal of the table of salaries issued by the Ministry will turn the thoughts of many Egyptian parents towards the adoption of the teaching profession for their sons.

        From this table we observe that L.E.16,360, which represents the sum specified in the Financial Adviser's Note for the improvement in the status of the staff, together with other money disposable for the same purpose, or rather more than half the total increase of the budget for 1905, will be devoted to advance the position of such officials as are engaged in school-work. To this substantial addition must also be added the increases of salary accorded by the Ministry each year in the month of January.

        Admission to the higher posts held by teachers is by means of the Training Colleges. Native inspectors and sub-inspectors of kuttabs are drawn from the Nasrieh Training College ; while attendance at the Training College for English-speaking natives opens the way to the headmasterships of Primary schools. We would particularly call attention to the greatly improved prospects of the headmasters and senior teachers in Primary schools.

        3.16. CAIRO DEACONESSES’ HOSPITAL.

        The many alterations and improvements that have been carried out during the past few months at the above hospital, and which it was hoped would have been completed by the 1st December last, will not be finished for another month or so, the delay having been caused by the native workmen employed. The whole of the masonry work in connection with the second floor that has been added to the hospital is now finished, and the plasterers, painters, and carpenters are busy completing the remaining work, but it is not intended to open the new wards before the 1st April, so as to allow time for them to dry thoroughly before admitting patients. In the mean while the old 1st and 2nd class wards on the first floor, which had been closed for some months during the building operations, have been reopened to paying patients. The new lift will also be in operation by the latter date.

        The additions to the building include fourteen rooms, which will be reserved exclusively in future for paying patients of European nationality, and the old wards will then be retained for natives. On the second storey there will be likewise a new special operating-room , fitted with all the latest improvements. This was needed in consequence of the great increase in the number of operations during the past few years.
